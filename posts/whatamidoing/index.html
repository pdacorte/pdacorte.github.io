Reminding Yourself

Recently I’ve been reading these posts which read something along the lines of “What would you do if you had 10x the agency”. And while the wording sounds silly to me, there is an exercise in there that is actually quite helpful.

For those that don’t know what they mean by agency, here’s a definition: (Personal) Agency refers to the exercise of individual control and autonomy in decision-making and actions

What should I be doing now?

This simple question, which prompts a mental repositioning, has proved useful to me in order to gain further clarity during any type of work.

In fact, most inner dialogue is helpful, as it prompts a more conscious and deep look into what I’m currently doing, or rather, should be doing.

So ask yourself: What should I be doing right now? And once you have the answer, you have your task. Go do it. Nothing feels better than getting something off your plate.

Learning Hard Things

Whenever one learns hard subjects or complex systems, one has to break them down into sizable bits. One key aspect of making things smaller is bringing the focus to them. It’s hard to learn coding, but it’s much simpler to learn what a data type is, what a function is, etc. Similarly, in an RTS game, it’s hard to simply “improve”, but it’s much easier to learn hotkeys, then an opening build order, then army compositions, steadily improving your skill.

Websites are just chunks of “views” propped up together. This is a way of simplifying web design which is helpful, as it alleviates a lot of the complexity usually associated with it.

Do Less

And despite all the things you want to learn, all the projects you could be doing, your time and attention are very limited, much more than one would like to admit. So do less, focus on what matters, and fill those limited slots with something worth filling.
